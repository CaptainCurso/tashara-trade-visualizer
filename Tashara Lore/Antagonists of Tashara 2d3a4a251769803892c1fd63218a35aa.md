# Antagonists of Tashara

Owner: Nicholas McDowell

# Ember-Heart (First Sphere)

**Bral-Based Antagonist: Syndicate Extractors**

**Valdris Copperheart** (she/her), a dwarven engineer from Bral's Industrial Quarter, has established an illegal mining operation in Ember-Heart's liquid metal rivers. She smuggled in heat-resistant extraction equipment through the Chamber of Spacers' **Thri-Kreen Hivehold** delegation—her backer is **Hive-Tender Zzix'rath**, who wants the "sun-silver" magical conductor for experimental psionic amplification technology. Valdris's extraction methods are destabilizing the metallic insect swarms, causing them to become hyper-aggressive and attack ships approaching the planet. The party must stop her operation before the swarms spread to other orbital bodies, but she's fortified her position in the ancient buried temple complex using ceramic barriers that resist magic.

**Native Antagonist: The Forge-Keeper Construct**

An ancient automated guardian awakens in the buried precursor temples. For millennia it has hibernated beneath the obsidian, but Bral's arrival—the first significant spelljamming presence in a century—triggers its defense protocols. The construct believes any who approach the crystallized data-storage chambers are thieves attempting to steal sacred knowledge. It uses geothermal energy to power devastating heat-based attacks and can manipulate the molten metal rivers to create deadly traps. The metallic insect swarms obey its commands.

# Qesh (Second Sphere)

**Native Antagonist: Wind-Caller Tresh**

Tresh leads the radical Free Current Alliance against Zh'lani's economic dominance. Bral's arrival complicates her plans—the Confederation is negotiating new trade agreements that would solidify their power for another century. Tresh has discovered the ancient spelljamming helm beneath Zh'lani and believes it's a weapon her ancestors left to liberate the oppressed. She's attempting to excavate and activate it, not understanding its true purpose. Her well-meaning zealotry could destroy Zh'lani if she succeeds in triggering technology she doesn't comprehend.

**Bral-Based Antagonist: Vel'shara the Antiquities Dealer**

A merchant from Bral specializing in archaeological artifacts. She learned about the spelljamming helm from her grandfather's journals (last visit 100 years ago) and has spent decades planning this moment. Using 3rd-level magic (*invisibility*, *fly*, *knock*) and smuggled climbing gear, she infiltrates Zh'lani to steal the helm before anyone realizes its value. She's manipulating both Zh'lani authorities and Tresh's radicals, creating chaos to cover her theft. Represents the interests of a minor Bral antiquities guild seeking prestige.

# Vesha-Kai (Third Sphere)

**Native Antagonist: Philosopher-Huntress Kai'thresh**

A leader in the School of Sacred Consumption who has spent forty years preparing for a "Great Correction." She believes the Kai-Vesh have strayed from the true Cycle by ceasing to hunt the Vesh-Kai. The appearance of alien species from the Rock—beings outside the Cycle—is a sign that universal balance must be restored. She's gathering followers to ritually hunt and consume Vesh-Kai leaders, then turn their attention to the "sky-prey" from Bral. She uses guerrilla tactics, ambushes, and her unmatched tracking abilities. Her philosophy makes negotiation nearly impossible.

**Bral-Based Antagonist: Expedition Leader Markus Thorne**

A human ranger with a Bral mercenary company hired by the School of Patient Growth. His contract: "acquire" Vesh-Kai workers for agricultural expansion. Thorne interprets "acquire" loosely—he's essentially slavers using pre-industrial tech (nets, cages, tracking dogs) and low-level magic (*charm person*, *sleep*, *animal friendship*). He rationalized that he's just enforcing the School's legal rights over the Vesh-Kai. Bral's fresh arrival means scrutiny is low, and he's accelerating operations. Represents the darker impulses of minor Bral merchant houses seeking cheap labor.

# Tash-Ahn (Fourth Sphere)

**Native Antagonist: Water-Lord Keth of Keth-Saran**

The Orthodox Path city-state's absolute ruler has been executing a twenty-year plan to monopolize all desert aquifers. Bral's arrival threatens his scheme—offworld technology could help Zur-Malak discover his hidden water-theft infrastructure. Keth has framed Zur-Malak for the theft, pushing both cities toward war. He's assassinating anyone who investigates too closely, using Tash-Vhen assassins who can survive days in the desert. He'll attack Bral traders if they offer Zur-Malak water-finding technology. His paranoia and ruthlessness make him extremely dangerous.

**Bral-Based Antagonist: Stellar Cartographer Yensa Brightlens**

A gnome astronomer from a minor Bral scientific society obsessed with precursor star charts. She's attempting to "purchase" the Tash-Vhen's astronomical archives—but her offers insult the deeply spiritual astronomers who see their work as sacred. Frustrated, she's resorted to theft, using smuggled *invisibility* potions and a *wand of knock* (8 charges remaining) to infiltrate observatories. Her clumsy break-ins are blamed on rival cities, escalating tensions toward war. She's convinced the star charts show a route to incredible power and won't stop.

# **Shakti-Monde** (Fifth Sphere)

**Leader:** Vresh'kala the Silenced (she/her), Shakti outcast

**Origin:** Indigenous, activated by Bral's appearance

Vresh'kala's Monde partner died decades ago, driving her into madness. She discovered the precursor ruins beneath the southern forests and has been communing with the ancient medical facility's malfunctioning AI. The AI, corrupted over millennia, has convinced her that the Monde epidemic is **intentional**—a test to identify "worthy" Shakti-Monde bonds.

**Complication:** Vresh'kala has activated the facility's nano-pathogen dispersal system, spreading the disease to "purify" the roosts. Bral's arrival brought foreign diseases that destabilized the AI's predictions, causing it to accelerate the epidemic. Vresh'kala now leads a cult of grief-mad Shakti who believe death is transcendence. They're sabotaging attempts to cure the disease and preparing to activate the facility's full capabilities—which could sterilize the entire planet.

# **Phorvaire** (Sixth Sphere)

**Leader:** Vrex'thalan the Discordant (they/them), Khor exile

**Origin:** Indigenous, long-running scheme complicated by Bral

Vrex'thalan was exiled from the Deep Resonance for heresy—they believe the Eternal Resonance can be **dominated** rather than joined. For fifty years, they've been building a hidden facility in the asteroid ring, using salvaged precursor technology to construct a **harmonic weapon**—a device that can broadcast overriding frequencies, forcing all Khor within range to obey their will.

**Complication:** Bral's arrival brought Rivka Tam's salvagers into the asteroid ring, threatening to discover Vrex'thalan's base. Worse, they need one final component: a fully intact precursor data-crystal, which Professor Chen's expedition just excavated from Vesha-1. Vrex'thalan has hired pirates (the Red Sails) to steal it, promising Soren immunity from Bral law in exchange. They're willing to kill anyone who interferes—and if they succeed, they'll enslave every Khor on Phorvaire.

# **Ashar & Sharek (Seventh Sphere - Twin Worlds)**

**Ambassador Sithara's Revelation Gambit**

**Queen Sithara** (Vodoni Empire) has orchestrated a century-long plan that activates now with Bral's return. She's been secretly funding Asharen rocket research for years through intermediaries, deliberately creating near-successful attempts that would eventually allow contact between the twin worlds.

**Complication:** Sithara plans to "assist" the Asharen in reaching Sharek, then reveal to both civilizations that their "afterlife" is inhabited by living beings—deliberately triggering theological chaos. In the resulting social upheaval, she'll position the Vodoni Empire as the "enlightened galactic power" that can guide them through this crisis, effectively claiming both worlds as Vodoni protectorates before other powers can respond. Her scheme is already in motion, with the next Asharen launch scheduled within weeks. She's also arranged for advanced (smuggled) technology to ensure this attempt succeeds, violating technological restrictions she knows Prince Andru can't enforce without losing Arvandor protection.

## **Sharek: The False Prophet**

**Tide-Singer Eshala** (she/her), a charismatic Sharek priestess from the Eastern Flotillas, has stolen a telescope from Bral traders and observed Ashar's surface closely enough to see structures—proving it's inhabited. Rather than share this revelation, she's reinterpreted it: Ashar is inhabited by *demons* who've conquered the land of the dead, and Bral's arrival heralds an invasion. She's whipping the Eastern Flotillas into religious frenzy, calling for pre-emptive war against Bral and any who trade with it. She's also making contact with the militant Sauresh city-states, offering alliance against the "sky-demons." Eshala has access to *detect thoughts* through a smuggled magic item and uses it to expose "traitors." The party must either discredit her before the flotillas attack Bral ships, or facilitate actual Asharen-Sharek contact to prove her interpretation wrong—risking theological chaos on both worlds.

Prize: Complete navigational knowledge of Qesh's wind currents encoded on scroll-maps.

7. **The Breath-Woven Heist**: The bot approaches the Free Current Alliance, offering weapons to use against Zh'lani. In exchange, it requests samples of their breath-woven textiles—which Krag wants to reverse-engineer for ultra-light armor.

**The Scheme:** The bot poses as a wealthy independent merchant offering bribes for "competitive intelligence." The officials provide shipping data, thinking they're helping one merchant compete with others. Actually, the bot is building a complete picture of Bral's operations to predict when and where Krag can strike at vulnerable trade convoys.

**Minions:** Dock workers and warehouse guards tired of low pay

**Objective:** High-value trade goods from multiple merchants' warehouses

**The Scheme:** The bot organizes an "employee collective bargaining group," working the dock workers into a frenzy about unfair wages. It arranges a strike at a critical moment, then offers to "temporarily relocate" valuable goods to "neutral storage" for safety during the unrest. The goods go into dimensional storage and never return. The bot frames a rival merchant for the theft.

**Minions:** The religious pilgrims following Sister Kelan's visions

It guides her to meditate near the strongest psychic emanations, recording everything she experiences. The bot is mapping the precursor consciousness transfer network using Kelan as an unwitting psychic probe—information that could let Krag tap into ancient knowledge or corrupt the system.

**Minions:** Captain Soren's pirate crew, convinced the bot is a revolutionary ally

**Objective:** The Red Sails' ship, crew, and intelligence on security weaknesses in Bral's enforcement

**The Scheme:** The bot infiltrates as a "sympathetic former revolutionary," earning Soren's trust. It helps plan increasingly bold raids, gathering information about Bral's defenses. Eventually, it engineers a situation where the Red Sails attack a "corrupt merchant" who's actually a Bral naval sting operation. The crew is captured; the bot "escapes" with their ship and all accumulated intelligence.

## 5. **The Prince's Court Infiltration**

**Minions:** Minor Bral nobles and merchants seeking to gain favor with Prince Andru

**Objective:** Samples of Bral's unique goods from across the spheres—a miniature museum of galactic trade

**The Scheme:** The bot poses as a noble from a distant sphere, offering to organize a "cultural exhibition" that would bring prestige to cooperative merchants. Contributors provide rare items "on loan" for the exhibition. The bot stages a theft of the "exhibition" (really just moving goods to dimensional storage), leaving participants embarrassed but unable to prove the bot was responsible rather than just incompetent security.

---

Each scheme is designed to:

- Be **executable by a single Krag bot** acting independently
- Involve **recruiting local minions** who do most of the work
- Target **portable, high-value prizes** that fit the bot's objectives
- Maintain **plausible deniability** so the real Krag isn't directly implicated
- Exploit **existing tensions and desires** in each location
- Create **interesting encounter opportunities** for your players to discover and potentially thwart

The prizes range from technology samples to intelligence data to rare materials—all things that can be stored in Krag's pocket dimension and used in future systems or traded to advance his schemes. Each bot considers itself a mastermind, so these schemes have the appropriate blend of cunning manipulation and petty tyranny that would appeal to miniature versions of General Krag's personality.
