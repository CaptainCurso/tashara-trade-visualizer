## **PLANETARY SURVEY DATA**

## **PLANET ONE: "Ember-Heart"**

**Orbital Period:** 73 days | **Diameter:** 0.87 Toril-standard

**Spectrographic Analysis:** Extreme thermal signature. Surface composition: basaltic formations (92%), metallic compounds (6%), crystalline structures (2%). Atmospheric particulate analysis indicates iron, osmium, iridium, and unknown metallic compound exhibiting magical resonance properties.

**Scrying Observation (Single Cast):** Liquid metal rivers confirmed. Geometric subsurface structures detected—likely artificial, non-functional. No mobile life signatures in visual spectrum. Silicon-based extremophile ecosystems probable.

**Resource Assessment:** HIGH VALUE - Rare transition metals in abundance. Significant excavation hazard due to thermal conditions and geothermal instability.

**Intelligent Life:** None detected.

---

## **PLANET TWO: "Qesh"**

**Orbital Period:** 248 days | **Diameter:** 1.02 Toril-standard

**Spectrographic Analysis:** Oxygen-nitrogen atmosphere (breathable). Surface: 78% saline water (shallow), 22% volcanic pumice formations (mobile). Atmospheric moisture content exceptionally high. Wind patterns: predictable high-velocity currents (300+ mph) circling globe.

**Scrying Observation (Two Casts):** INTELLIGENT LIFE CONFIRMED. Multiple mobile settlements detected—structures appear biological/woven, mounted on drifting landmasses. Population estimate: 200,000-400,000 sentient beings (amphibious morphology suggested by dual aquatic/aerial infrastructure). Technology level: pre-industrial but sophisticated aerodynamic engineering.

**Resource Assessment:** MODERATE VALUE - Unique textiles visible in scrying (unusually light material), marine resources, pumice stone. Metal deposits: minimal.

**Trade Potential:** HIGH - Settlements exhibit organized structure consistent with trading civilizations. No hostile military infrastructure observed.

---

## **PLANET THREE: "Vesha-Kai"**

**Orbital Period:** 387 days | **Diameter:** 1.14 Toril-standard | **Moons:** 3

**Spectrographic Analysis:** Optimal atmospheric composition. Surface: 40% water, 60% landmass (forests 45%, savanna 30%, mountains 15%, other 10%). Rich biodiversity indicators. Three moons creating complex tidal patterns.

**Scrying Observation (Three Casts):** INTELLIGENT LIFE CONFIRMED - MULTIPLE SPECIES. Large insectoid bipeds (7-8 feet tall, arthropod physiology) observed in organized settlements—both surface cities and cliff-structures. Secondary smaller arboreal species (3 feet tall) detected in forest canopies. Population estimate: 300,000-500,000 combined. Technology: medieval-Renaissance level (metallurgy, printing evidence, advanced architecture). Relationship between species unclear—mixed settlements observed in some locations.

**Moon Survey:** Vesha-1 shows artificial structures (ruins, non-functional). Vesha-2 exhibits active surface operations (primitive mining?). Vesha-3 shrouded in dense aerosol layer (fungal spores?—unusual biological signatures).

**Resource Assessment:** HIGH VALUE - Biological specimens, pharmaceutical potential, hardwoods, metals. Unique armored materials observed.

**Trade Potential:** VERY HIGH - Advanced civilization with apparent philosophical/scholarly development. Non-aggressive infrastructure.

---

## **PLANET FOUR: "Tash-Ahn"**

**Orbital Period:** 521 days | **Diameter:** 1.23 Toril-standard | **Moon:** 1

**Spectrographic Analysis:** Extreme environmental diversity. Single supercontinent spanning pole-to-pole. Climate zones: arctic (poles), temperate mountains, central desert (43% of landmass), tropical coasts. Atmospheric clarity exceptional in desert regions.

**Scrying Observation (Four Casts):** INTELLIGENT LIFE CONFIRMED - THREE DISTINCT SPECIES.

- Desert: Reptilian humanoids (5 feet), organized oasis cities, sophisticated irrigation. Population ~200,000.
- Mountains: Large humanoids (6.5 feet), extensive altitude settlements carved into cliffs. Population ~150,000.
- Coasts: Amphibious humanoids, hybrid air/water architecture, advanced sailing vessels. Population ~300,000.
    
    Minimal inter-species contact observed. Each maintains separate territories. Technology: medieval (variable by species).
    

**Lunar Survey:** Ahn-Kor shows metallic debris in major crater—potential spacecraft wreckage (ancient). Crystalline mineral deposits detected with magical resonance properties.

**Resource Assessment:** VERY HIGH VALUE - Multiple resource bases (metals from mountains, glass/astronomy from desert, maritime goods from coast). Ancient salvage potential on moon.

**Trade Potential:** EXTREMELY HIGH - Three civilizations create arbitrage opportunities. Appear to have limited inter-species trade—intermediary services potentially lucrative.

---

## **PLANET FIVE: "Shakti-Monde"**

**Orbital Period:** 673 days | **Diameter:** 0.98 Toril-standard | **Moons:** 2

**Spectrographic Analysis:** Optimal conditions. Surface: 35% water, 65% landmass (ancient forests 55%, mountains 25%, grasslands 20%). Pronounced axial tilt (30°)—strong seasonal variation.

**Scrying Observation (Three Casts):** INTELLIGENT LIFE CONFIRMED - SYMBIOTIC SPECIES. Avian-descended bipeds (5 feet, vestigial wings) observed in consistent proximity to large flying mammals (horse-sized, bat-like). Settlement patterns: massive tree-cities and cliff installations. Population estimate: 400,000-600,000. Technology: developed medieval (metallurgy, papermaking). Frequent aerial activity—every observed individual travels via flying companion.

**Subsurface Anomaly:** Extensive artificial structures detected beneath southern forest settlements. Inactive but intact. Same architectural signature as ruins on other worlds.

**Resource Assessment:** HIGH VALUE - Unique biological materials (observed silk-like substance), aerial navigation knowledge, lightweight metallurgy. Volcanic forges active in mountain regions.

**Trade Potential:** HIGH - Organized settlements, apparent peaceful culture. Their aerial mobility may enable unique services.

---

## **PLANET SIX: "Phorvaire"**

**Orbital Period:** 891 days | **Diameter:** 1.31 Toril-standard | **Asteroid Ring:** Dense

**Spectrographic Analysis:** EXTREME HAZARD ENVIRONMENT. High volcanic activity. Atmosphere: breathable but sulfurous (45 ppm SO₂). Surface instability: constant seismic activity. Minimal water. Temperature extremes. Silicon-based life signatures detected.

**Scrying Observation (Two Casts):** INTELLIGENT LIFE CONFIRMED - CRYSTALLINE SILICON-BASED ORGANISMS. Humanoid crystalline beings (6 feet, translucent to opaque structures) observed in extensive underground cities. Advanced engineering evident—cities extend multiple miles underground. Population estimate: 200,000-300,000. Technology: early industrial (steam power observed, complex machinery, electrical-analog systems using crystal resonance).

**Asteroid Ring Survey:** Rich mineral deposits. Artificial structures on multiple large asteroids—ruins matching other system examples. One asteroid shows recent surface disturbance (salvage operations?).

**Resource Assessment:** EXTREMELY HIGH VALUE - Unique minerals unavailable elsewhere, advanced crystal technology (communication systems detected operating planet-wide), geological expertise, heat-resistant materials.

**Trade Potential:** MODERATE-HIGH - Advanced civilization but extreme environmental hostility. Surface operations not recommended. Orbital rendezvous required. Cultural xenophobia possible given isolation.

**CRITICAL NOTE:** Do not approach planet surface without atmospheric processors and thermal shielding. Recommend remote contact protocols.

---

## **PLANET SEVEN: "The Twin Worlds" - Binary Planet System**

**Orbital Period:** 1,247 days (around stars) | **Binary Orbit:** 31 days (around each other) | **Diameter:** 0.94 and 1.01 Toril-standard

**ASHAR (Inner Twin - Red World):**

**Spectrographic Analysis:** Thin atmosphere (breathable). Cold climate. Surface: 85% desert/rocky terrain, 15% polar ice. Minimal water. Iron oxide content high (red coloration).

**Scrying Observation (Two Casts):** INTELLIGENT LIFE CONFIRMED. Insectoid bipeds (4 feet, chitinous) in subterranean settlements extending deep underground. Surface installations: numerous astronomical towers (all oriented toward Sharek). Population estimate: 300,000-500,000. Technology: early industrial (steam engines detected, primitive rocketry attempts observed—all failures).

**SHAREK (Outer Twin - Blue World):**

**Spectrographic Analysis:** Optimal atmosphere. Warm climate. Surface: 65% water, 35% landmass (single supercontinent, dense jungle).

**Scrying Observation (Three Casts):** INTELLIGENT LIFE CONFIRMED - TWO SPECIES.

- Aquatic mammalians (5 feet, otter-like morphology suggested), extensive floating cities, advanced sailing vessels. Population ~300,000.
- Terrestrial reptilians (6 feet, bipedal), jungle settlements, city-state structures. Population ~200,000.
    
    Inter-species conflict observed (naval engagements detected). Technology: medieval-Renaissance.
    

**Lagrange Point Survey:** Debris field at gravitational equilibrium between twins. Mineral-rich asteroids. No active installations.

**Resource Assessment:** HIGH VALUE (both worlds) - Ashar: gemstones, excavation technology, explosives, astronomical data. Sharek: maritime resources, shipbuilding knowledge, jungle biologicals.

**Trade Potential:** VERY HIGH - Both worlds maintain organized civilizations.

**CULTURAL ANOMALY DETECTED:** Both worlds maintain astronomical installations obsessively focused on observing each other. Religious/theological significance probable. Recommend anthropological caution.

## **SYSTEM-WIDE OBSERVATIONS**

**Precursor Civilization Evidence:** Artificial structures detected on six of seven worlds (excluding Qesh) plus multiple moons and asteroids. Architectural consistency suggests single extinct civilization inhabited multiple bodies simultaneously. Ruins exhibit identical construction: dark stone (light-absorbing properties), crystalline data storage, astronomical alignment. Age estimate: 5,000-15,000 years. Salvage value: potentially extreme if data crystals functional.

**Space Hazards:**

- Sphere fracture zones emitting anomalous energy (magical signature)
- Large draconic life signatures detected near inner system (solar radiation feeders?)
- Incorporeal entities detected in outer system (void-based life?)
- Asteroid belt around Phorvaire: navigation hazard but resource-rich

**Non-Native Activity Detected:**

- Three vessels operating independently in system (spelljammers, non-Stardust)
- Small installations on Vesha-1 moon (recent construction)
- Salvage operations in Phorvaire asteroid belt (active)

## **RECOMMENDATIONS - PRIORITY ORDER**

**1. TASH-AHN** - Three civilizations, minimal inter-species trade, safe environment, high arbitrage potential. Ancient salvage on moon. Recommend first landing.

**2. VESHA-KAI** - Two species, advanced civilization, safe environment, biological value high. Moon ruins accessible. Strong secondary choice.

**3. SHAKTI-MONDE** - Unique symbiotic culture, aerial capability intriguing, subsurface ruins promising. Lower priority due to distance.

**4. TWIN WORLDS** - High complexity due to dual civilization and inter-species conflict on Sharek. Recommend later investigation after establishing system contacts.

**5. QESH** - Accessible, friendly infrastructure observed, but lower resource diversity. Good fallback option.

**6. PHORVAIRE** - Highest value but extreme hazard. Recommend only after acquiring environmental protection and establishing remote contact.

**7. EMBER-HEART** - No intelligent life. Resource extraction only, specialized equipment required.

---

**FINAL ASSESSMENT:** Tashara Sphere represents extraordinary commercial opportunity. Multiple pre-industrial civilizations with complementary resource bases and minimal inter-world contact create ideal conditions for intermediary trade operations. Ancient ruins present across system offer potential archaeological/technological windfall. Recommend cautious but prompt engagement.

**System Threat Level:** LOW-MODERATE. No hostile military forces detected. Primary risks: environmental hazards, cultural misunderstandings, competition from other vessels.
