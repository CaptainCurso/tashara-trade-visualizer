# The Tashara Sphere: Gateway of Forgotten Empires

Owner: Nicholas McDowell

The Rock of Bral emerges from the shimmering curtain of the Astral Sea into the Tashara Sphere with practiced regularity, its irregular orbit through the galaxy bringing it here every 37 standard years. This crystal sphere lies far from the Lattice of Heaven—the pholstiron Flow that connects most civilized systems—making it a backwater preserved in cultural amber, its inhabitants developing in isolation save for the periodic arrival of Bral's eclectic merchants. For the Stardust, fresh from their encounter with the legendary trading asteroid, this represents their first opportunity to explore, trade, and potentially resupply in relative safety.

The sphere itself is unusual. Its crystal shell bears visible scars—great fracture lines that glow faintly amber in the light of the system's twin suns. These cracks don't breach the shell but suggest some ancient cataclysm that the sphere survived. Tortle astronomers on the Rock hypothesize these scars predate current civilizations by millennia.

## The Tashara System: Twin Suns and Seven Worlds

## Primary Bodies

**Tashara-Major and Tashara-Minor** form a binary star system, two yellow suns locked in a slow gravitational dance. Their combined light bathes the system in a warm, golden-amber glow that shifts subtly as the stars revolve around their common center. This double illumination creates no true night on the inhabited worlds—only "dim hours" when one sun sets before the other rises, casting everything in perpetual twilight.youtube

The orbital mechanics create complex seasonal patterns. Each world experiences four distinct seasons as it passes through different positions relative to both stars: the Brightening (both suns visible), the First Dimming (Major sets, Minor remains), the Deepening (Minor sets, Major remains), and the Second Dimming (the brief true twilight when both are low). These cycles have profoundly shaped local cultures, philosophies, and agriculture.

## The Seven Worlds

## **First Sphere: Ember-Heart**

Closest to the binary stars, Ember-Heart is a scorched world of obsidian plains and rivers of liquid metal. Its atmosphere is breathable but stiflingly hot and laden with metallic vapors. The planet's surface is dominated by vast basaltic formations that erupt regularly with geysers of superheated gas, creating forests of crystallized minerals.

No intelligent civilization exists here, but the planet hosts extremophile ecosystems of silicon-based organisms—crystalline serpents that "swim" through solid rock, colonies of heat-drinking fungi that glow with bioluminescence, and vast swarms of metallic insects that harvest minerals from the air. The planet's dangers include unpredictable geothermal explosions, toxic atmospheric pockets, and gravitational anomalies near the molten metal rivers.

**Archaeological Opportunity:** Deep scanning reveals massive geometric structures buried beneath the obsidian—ancient temples or monuments from a civilization that either adapted to or fled the planet's increasing heat. The structures are made of an unknown ceramic that resists all known forms of divination magic. Excavating them would require heat-resistant equipment and significant time, but they contain chambers filled with crystallized data-storage devices that glow with residual magical energy. These could hold astronomical data, magical formulae, or historical records worth a fortune to the right buyer.

**Resource Potential:** The liquid metal rivers contain rare transition metals in high concentrations—osmium, iridium, and something the locals call "sun-silver" that appears to be a naturally occurring magical conductor. However, harvesting requires specialized heat-shielding and risks attracting the metallic insect swarms, which become aggressive when their feeding grounds are disturbed.

## **Second Sphere: Qesh**

Qesh is a world of endless wind. Its surface is dominated by vast, shallow seas and archipelagos of floating pumice islands that drift on the currents. **The Qeshi People:** The Qeshi are an amphibious species evolved from something between newts and flying fish—slender bodies with both gills and lungs, four limbs ending in webbed hands, and membranous wings that allow them to glide on the powerful winds. Their skin displays bioluminescent patterns that shift with emotion and communication, creating a visual language as complex as their spoken tongue. They are roughly four feet tall and have large, expressive eyes adapted to see through both water and mist.

The Qeshi civilization is built around the wind rivers. Their cities are magnificent structures of woven kelp-fiber and lightweight volcanic stone, designed to catch the wind and actually move with the pumice islands they're built upon. Each city is essentially a massive ship, guided by sail-tenders who adjust enormous membrane sails to navigate the global wind currents. **Culture and Technology:** The Qeshi have developed a sophisticated understanding of aerodynamics and fluid dynamics but remain pre-industrial, relying on wind and water power. Their society is organized around "Current-Families"—extended kinship groups that maintain specific sections of a city's sail system. Social status is determined by one's ability to read the wind and predict its changes, with the greatest navigators serving as both political and spiritual leaders.

The Qeshi worship the Breath of the Twin—a cosmic wind they believe flows from the dance of Tashara-Major and Minor through all things. Their religious observances involve group gliding ceremonies during the dim hours, when the winds are calmest. They have developed a rich tradition of wind-songs—complex melodies created by carefully shaped wooden instruments that can be heard across vast distances.

A third faction, the **Still-Water Seekers**, represents radical elements who want to anchor the cities permanently and abandon the nomadic lifestyle.

## **Third Sphere: Vesha-Kai**

Vesha-Kai is a verdant paradise, a world of deep forests, wide savannas, and crystalline rivers. It has three moons—Vesha-1, Vesha-2, and Vesha-3, locally called the Three Sisters—which create complex tidal patterns in the planet's numerous seas. The climate is temperate to tropical, with distinct wet and dry seasons influenced by the planet's orbit around the binary stars.youtube

**The Kai-Vesh:**

The Kai-Vesh are a species of sentient mantis-folk—standing seven to eight feet tall with four arms, compound eyes that can see into the infrared spectrum, and chitinous exoskeletons that range from deep green to golden amber. They are obligate carnivores but have developed a sophisticated ethical framework around hunting and death. Their religious philosophy, called the Cycle of Necessary Sacrifice, teaches that all life feeds on life, and the greatest honor is to be eaten by a greater predator, thus continuing the cycle. They practice ritual cannibalism of their honored dead as a form of reverence.

**Culture and Society:**

The Kai-Vesh civilization is organized into philosophical schools rather than geographic nations. Each school represents a different interpretation of the Cycle and its implications for society. These schools maintain territories but individuals can move between them, and mixed-school communities are common. The major schools include:

**The School of the Swift Hunt:** Believes the Cycle demands excellence in predation—hunting should be quick, efficient, and honorable. They've developed extraordinary tracking abilities and hunting techniques. Their territory covers the vast savanna of the northern continent, where they hunt the massive six-legged herbivores that migrate seasonally. They are the most martial of the schools and maintain a warrior tradition.

**The School of Patient Growth:** Focuses on agriculture and animal husbandry, arguing that shaping prey populations is the highest form of participation in the Cycle. They've created extensive farming complexes in the fertile river valleys, cultivating massive insect colonies for food and developing complex irrigation systems. They are the most technologically advanced school, having developed sophisticated pottery, metalworking, and engineering.

**The School of Sacred Consumption:** The religious fundamentalists, they believe every meal is a sacrament and have developed elaborate rituals around killing and eating. They maintain the great temple-cities in the deep forests, where they study the spiritual aspects of the Cycle. They are scholars and priests, and their libraries contain centuries of philosophical writings.

**The School of the Final Rest:** A smaller, more radical school that argues the Cycle can be transcended through voluntary death-offering. They maintain mortuary complexes where the elderly or terminally ill come to be ritually consumed by the school's members, who believe they take on the wisdom and memories of the deceased. This practice disturbs even other Kai-Vesh.

**Multiple Species:** Vesha-Kai is also home to a second intelligent species, the **Vesh-Kai** (the naming is the source of much confusion). These are a species of small, tree-dwelling primates—about three feet tall, with prehensile tails and opposable thumbs on all four limbs. They are omnivorous tool-users who live in the great forest canopies. Their relationship with the Kai-Vesh is complex and uncomfortable.

Historically, the Vesh-Kai were prey species for the Kai-Vesh. As the Kai-Vesh developed philosophical frameworks around hunting, they began to recognize signs of intelligence in the Vesh-Kai—tool use, language, and complex social structures. This created a profound ethical crisis: if the Vesh-Kai are sentient, does hunting them violate the principles of honorable predation? Different schools reached different conclusions.

The School of the Swift Hunt and School of Sacred Consumption ceased hunting Vesh-Kai, recognizing them as fellow participants in the Cycle rather than prey. The School of Patient Growth attempted to incorporate them as agricultural workers, leading to a system uncomfortably close to slavery. The School of the Final Rest briefly advocated for ritual consumption of willing Vesh-Kai volunteers, a practice that ended after widespread revulsion.

Currently, the Vesh-Kai exist in a liminal state—recognized as sentient by most Kai-Vesh but without full social equality. They maintain their own communities in the forest canopies, engage in trade with the Kai-Vesh, and some have even joined Kai-Vesh philosophical schools. However, tensions remain high, particularly in School of Patient Growth territories where older practices persist.

The Vesh-Kai have developed their own culture centered on storytelling, craftsmanship, and what they call "the Weaving"—an intricate understanding of how ecosystems interconnect. They create stunning textiles, wood carvings, and musical instruments. Their oral histories contain knowledge of Vesha-Kai's ecology that the Kai-Vesh lack. Some of the most innovative developments in Kai-Vesh society have come from Vesh-Kai engineers.

**Technology Level:** The Kai-Vesh have reached late medieval to early Renaissance technology—they have metallurgy, the printing press, advanced mathematics, and early chemistry. Their architecture is distinctive: massive structures of stone and chitin-reinforced mortar, with soaring vertical elements designed for their climbing abilities. They have no gunpowder or similar explosives, as their philosophical traditions have channeled innovation toward other areas.

**Limited Spelljamming:** The Kai-Vesh have legends of their ancestors arriving from the stars, but no functional spelljamming capability. They have built primitive rockets for their three moons using controlled chemical reactions, but these are dangerous and unreliable. Only the School of Patient Growth has successfully sent unmanned probes to the Three Sisters. They desperately want to explore their moons but lack the magical or technological means.youtube

**Trade Opportunities:**

The Kai-Vesh are hungry for knowledge of other worlds and other species—how do they organize themselves? What are their philosophies around life and death? They offer:

- **Chitin-Forged Armor:** Lightweight, durable armor made from treated Kai-Vesh carapaces (donated upon death)
- **Philosophical Texts:** Their libraries contain sophisticated ethical frameworks that could influence developing civilizations
- **Biological Specimens:** They have encyclopedic knowledge of their planet's ecosystems and can provide rare plants, insects, and genetic samples
- **Tracking Services:** Kai-Vesh hunters are unmatched trackers and could assist in planetary exploration
- **Agricultural Innovations:** The School of Patient Growth has developed farming techniques that could revolutionize food production

**What They Need:**

- Star charts and information about other worlds
- Magical healing (they have effective medicine but no magical tradition)
- Metal ores (they have limited deposits)
- Communication devices (coordinating between schools across continents is difficult)
- Books on alien species and cultures
- Gold Presses (GP) from previous Bral visits

**Dangers:** The savannas and forests of Vesha-Kai are home to apex predators that the Kai-Vesh themselves fear—massive reptilian creatures they call Star-Fangs, which hunt in packs and have rudimentary intelligence. Some philosophers argue Star-Fangs may be on the cusp of sentience, which would create another ethical crisis. ### **The Three Sisters (Vesha-1, Vesha-2, Vesha-3):**

The moons of Vesha-Kai offer their own opportunities and dangers:

**Vesha-1 (The Hunter's Moon):** The largest moon, with a thin but breathable atmosphere. Its surface is covered in impact craters and vast fields of a crystal that the Kai-Vesh call "sky-glass." Archaeological evidence suggests this moon was once inhabited—ruined structures of unfamiliar architecture dot the landscape. The ruins are currently being excavated by School of Patient Growth researchers, who have found artifacts suggesting the previous inhabitants had advanced astronomical knowledge. However, the excavation teams have reported disturbing phenomena—sounds with no source, equipment malfunctions, and several researchers disappearing during the planet's dim hours.

**Vesha-2 (The Grower's Moon):** A smaller moon with no atmosphere but rich mineral deposits. The School of Patient Growth has established a mining outpost here using primitive spacesuits (essentially treated leather and resin sealed chambers). The operation is incredibly dangerous—several miners have died—but the moon contains deposits of a magnetic ore the Kai-Vesh use in their most advanced engineering. A successful trade relationship could provide access to this ore, or the Stardust could offer superior mining technology in exchange for a share of the profits

**Vesha-3 (The Sister of Mysteries):** The most distant moon, perpetually shrouded in a thick, cloud-like mist that defies all attempts at penetration by telescope. No Kai-Vesh expedition has successfully landed there—the few rockets that reached it lost all communication upon entering the mist and were never recovered. Rumors circulate that Vesha-3 is hollow, inhabited by ghosts, or serves as a prison for some ancient horror. The truth is unknown, making it an irresistible target for bold explorers.

**Scientific Interest:** The Stardust's sensors could reveal what the Kai-Vesh cannot see—Vesha-3's mist is actually a dense spore cloud from an enormous fungal organism that has colonized the entire moon. The fungus appears to be sentient in some collective way, and its spores interfere with electronic and magical communications. ## **Fourth Sphere: Tash-Ahn**

Tash-Ahn is a world of extremes—vast deserts, towering mountain ranges, and deep ocean trenches. It has a single massive continent spanning from pole to pole, with narrow seas on either side. The climate varies dramatically with latitude, from frozen tundra to scorching desert to tropical jungle. This environmental diversity has led to the development of three distinct civilizations, each adapted to different biomes.youtube

**The Three Civilizations:**

**The Tash-Vhen (Desert People):**

The Tash-Vhen are a humanoid species with reptilian characteristics—scaled skin, vertical pupils, and the ability to enter a state of torpor during extreme heat. They are shorter than humans, averaging five feet tall, with elongated fingers and toes that help them move across sand. They have a third eyelid that protects against sandstorms and can survive days without water.

The Tash-Vhen civilization is built around oasis cities in the great central desert. Each city controls a network of underground aquifers and has developed sophisticated irrigation systems. Their society is organized around water rights—families own specific shares of their city's water supply, and wealth is measured in water-hours (the time one can irrigate one's crops). They have developed a complex legal system around water management, with specialized courts that handle disputes.

The Tash-Vhen are master astronomers. Their clear desert skies and the binary stars' predictable patterns have allowed them to develop accurate calendars and navigation by stars. They've mapped not just Tashara's system but nearby stellar patterns, creating star charts that could be valuable for spelljamming. However, they interpret these patterns through religious frameworks—they believe the stars are the eyes of ancestral spirits watching over them.

**Technology and Culture:** The Tash-Vhen have developed glassmaking to an art form, using the desert sand to create everything from simple vessels to complex lenses for their telescopes. Their architecture features elaborate windcatchers and underground cooling systems. They have metallurgy but metal is scarce, so they use it sparingly for tools and weapons. Their society is stratified—water-wealthy families control politics, while water-poor families work as laborers.

They worship **Tash-Ahn the Unifier**, a legendary hero who supposedly taught them to find water underground and united the warring desert tribes. Different cities emphasize different aspects of Tash-Ahn's teachings, leading to theological disputes. The city of **Keth-Saran** follows the Orthodox Path, maintaining traditional water laws strictly. The city of **Zur-Malak** practices the Reformed Path, experimenting with communal water ownership. Tensions between these two cities have escalated recently, with accusations of water theft and threats of war.

**The Ahn-Sharek (Mountain People):**

The Ahn-Sharek are a different species entirely—large, stocky humanoids averaging six and a half feet tall, with thick hair covering most of their bodies and adaptations for high altitude living. They have enlarged lungs and hearts, allowing them to function in thin air that would leave other species gasping. Their skin has a bluish tint due to unique blood chemistry.

The Ahn-Sharek live in the towering mountains that ring Tash-Ahn's southern polar region. Their settlements are carved directly into cliff faces and mountainsides, connected by elaborate bridge systems and climbing paths. They are master stonemasons and engineers, creating structures that seem to defy gravity. Their society is organized around clan-holds, each controlling a mountain or range.

The Ahn-Sharek have no written language—their history and knowledge are passed down through elaborate song-cycles that can take days to perform. Each clan maintains their own version of the Great Song, which serves as history, law, and religious text combined. Disputes between clans are resolved through song-competitions, where representatives try to prove their clan's version of the Song is more accurate. This system maintains peace but makes coordination difficult.

**Technology and Culture:** The Ahn-Sharek have developed advanced rope-making, using fibers from mountain plants to create incredibly strong cables. They've also mastered controlled avalanche techniques, using them for mining and warfare. Their metallurgy is excellent—the mountains provide rich ore deposits—and they trade metal goods to the Tash-Vhen and Vhen-Dok for things they lack (primarily food).

They worship the **Peaks of Forever**, believing that the highest mountains are the bones of dead gods who hold up the sky. Climbing is a religious act—the higher one climbs, the closer to divinity. The most sacred site is the **Summit of All Voices**, the tallest mountain on Tash-Ahn, which no climber has successfully summited. Expeditions that attempt it tend not to return, but this only adds to its mystique.

**The Vhen-Dok (Coastal People):**

The Vhen-Dok are an aquatic species similar to the Tash-Vhen but adapted for marine environments—they have gills as well as lungs, webbed hands and feet, and can survive in both saltwater and freshwater. They have developed both underwater and above-water settlements along the coasts, with elaborate tide-systems for moving between them.

The Vhen-Dok are master sailors and shipbuilders. Their vessels range from simple canoes to massive multi-hulled ships that can transport hundreds. They've explored every coast of Tash-Ahn and have begun tentative trading relationships with both the Tash-Vhen (trading fish and salt for glass and metal) and the Ahn-Sharek (trading coral and shells for metal tools and weapons).

Their society is organized around **wave-clans**—extended families that crew specific ships and claim specific fishing territories. Social status is determined by the size and speed of one's ship and the distant one has sailed. The greatest achievement is to sail around the entire continent, a journey taking years. Those who complete it are called **World-Sailors** and are revered as living heroes.

**Technology and Culture:** The Vhen-Dok have developed sophisticated navigation techniques using currents, winds, and star positions. They've also developed underwater construction techniques, building structures that can withstand ocean pressure. Their society is matriarchal—ship captains are traditionally female, and inheritance passes through the mother's line.

They worship **Vhen the Eternal Current**, a deity they believe flows through all water and connects all seas. Their religious practice involves offerings thrown into the ocean during specific tide-phases. They believe the dead join the Current and flow forever through the world's waters. Some wave-clans practice water burial—wrapping the deceased in weighted shrouds and consigning them to the deep.

**Recent Developments:** About fifty years ago, a Vhen-Dok expedition discovered the ruins of an ancient civilization on a remote island. These ruins are unlike anything built by the Tash-Vhen, Ahn-Sharek, or Vhen-Dok—they're constructed from a strange black stone that seems to absorb light, and they're covered in indecipherable writing. The Vhen-Dok have kept the island's location secret, fearing other species would try to claim it, but word has leaked. Both Tash-Vhen cities and several Ahn-Sharek clans are trying to discover the island's location, creating a potential crisis.

**Inter-Species Relations:**

The three species of Tash-Ahn are aware of each other but have limited contact. The Tash-Vhen view the Ahn-Sharek as barbaric and the Vhen-Dok as uncivilized. The Ahn-Sharek view the Tash-Vhen as weak and obsessed with water-grubbing, and they barely understand the Vhen-Dok's amphibious nature. The Vhen-Dok think the Tash-Vhen are parched fools and the Ahn-Sharek are mad to live where the air is thin. However, increasing trade is slowly breaking down these prejudices.

**Limited Spelljamming:** None of the three species has developed spelljamming. The Tash-Vhen's astronomy is sophisticated but theoretical—they've never conceived of actually traveling to the stars. The Ahn-Sharek don't even think about the sky beyond what they can see from their peaks. The Vhen-Dok have legends of sailing the "Sky-Sea" but consider it a metaphor rather than possibility.

### **Tash-Ahn's Moon - Ahn-Kor:**

Tash-Ahn has a single large moon called Ahn-Kor (each species has a different name for it, but this is the Tash-Vhen term, which is most commonly used in scholarship). It has no atmosphere and a heavily cratered surface. The Tash-Vhen have telescopes powerful enough to see surface features and have created detailed maps, but they've never conceived of actually visiting it.

Ahn-Kor is valuable for several reasons. First, it contains deposits of a rare crystalline mineral that resonates with magical energy—effectively, raw material for creating magic items or even modified spelljamming helms. Second, one of its larger craters contains the wreckage of an ancient spelljamming vessel, preserved in perfect vacuum for thousands of years. The ship is of a design unknown to current galactic civilizations and could provide valuable technological insights. However, salvaging it would require a moon landing, extended EVA operations, and potentially dangerous investigation of unknown technology.

## **Fifth Sphere: Shakti-Monde**

Shakti-Monde is the jewel of the Tashara Sphere—a world of stunning beauty and complex ecosystems. It has a single supercontinent surrounded by vast oceans, with climate zones ranging from tropical rainforests to temperate grasslands. The planet has a 30-degree axial tilt, creating pronounced seasons, and two small moons that create mild tides.

**The Shakti:**

The Shakti are an avian species—evolved from something like parrots or cockatoos, standing about five feet tall with vestigial wings that no longer allow flight but still serve for display and communication. Their bodies are covered in colorful plumage that varies by region, and they have mobile beaks capable of fine manipulation. Their hands are actually modified wing-claws, with three opposable digits.

What makes the Shakti unique is their symbiotic relationship with a species of flying mammals called **Monde**. The Monde are large creatures—about the size of horses—with bat-like wings and prehensile tails. They're intelligent but not fully sentient, roughly equivalent to dolphins. Shakti and Monde have evolved together over millions of years, developing a bond that goes beyond simple domestication.

Every Shakti child is paired with a Monde juvenile in a bonding ceremony around age five. This bond lasts for life—the Shakti and Monde are rarely separated, communicating through a combination of calls, pheromones, and something the Shakti describe as "heart-knowing." Bonded Monde will die of grief if their Shakti partner dies, and Shakti whose Monde die often fall into deep depression or suicide. This symbiosis has shaped every aspect of Shakti culture.

**Culture and Society:**

Shakti civilization is built around **roosts**—enormous tree-cities in the planet's ancient forests and cliff-side settlements in the mountains. These roosts can house tens of thousands of Shakti and their Monde partners. Architecture is designed for aerial approach—platforms at various heights, spiral ramps rather than stairs, and open spaces for Monde to maneuver.

Shakti society is egalitarian and consensus-based. Decisions are made through roost-songs—communal singing sessions where every adult voice is heard and everyone works to create harmony. The metaphor is literal—a roost has achieved proper consensus when their individual songs blend into a perfect chord. This makes decision-making slow but ensures everyone's needs are considered.

The Shakti have no concept of property ownership beyond personal items. Roosts share food, shelter, and resources communally. This sometimes causes confusion when trading with outsiders who expect normal economic transactions. However, they do understand exchange—they're willing to provide goods and services in return for things they value, but the negotiation style is very different from typical haggling.

**Multiple Nations:** While Shakti culture is relatively unified compared to other species in the Tashara Sphere, there are distinct cultural regions based on geography and bonding practices:

**The Forest Roosts** (Southern Forests): These Shakti bond with **Tree-Monde**, a subspecies adapted for maneuvering through dense canopy. They're smaller and more agile than other Monde varieties. Forest Roosts are the most traditional, maintaining ancient customs and songs. # **Sixth Sphere: Phorvaire**

Phorvaire is a harsh world—a planet of volcanic activity, toxic gas vents, and unstable tectonic plates. Its atmosphere is technically breathable but laden with sulfurous compounds that make prolonged exposure painful. The planet is in a constant state of geological upheaval, with new mountains rising and old ones collapsing, lava flows rewriting the landscape regularly, and earth-quakes so common the inhabitants barely notice them.youtube

**The Khor:**

The Khor are a species of silicon-based life—beings whose bodies are primarily crystalline structures infused with organic compounds. They're humanoid in shape, standing about six feet tall, with bodies that range from translucent quartz to opaque obsidian depending on their diet and age. They don't have traditional organs—instead, their bodies are lattices of crystal that conduct energy and information through resonance. They can survive in environments that would kill carbon-based life—intense heat, toxic atmospheres, even brief exposure to vacuum.

The Khor "eat" by absorbing minerals—they literally consume rocks, which their bodies break down and incorporate into their crystalline structure. They don't sleep in a traditional sense but enter a state of low-energy resonance for a few hours each day. They reproduce through crystallization—a parent Khor will grow a small crystal seed that eventually breaks off and begins independent life. This process takes years and is considered sacred.

**Culture and Society:**

Khor society is organized around **resonance-groups**—collections of Khor whose crystalline structures harmonize at similar frequencies. The Khor have developed a unique form of democracy. Through this process, they literally feel the collective will of their people and make decisions that create the strongest harmonic resonance. This system prevents tyranny but makes rapid decision-making impossible.

### **Ashar: The Red World**

Its atmosphere is thin but breathable, and its climate is cold—temperatures rarely rise above comfortable levels, and nights can be lethally frigid. The planet has a single supercontinent divided by mountain ranges and vast dust-bowl basins.

**The Asharen:**

The Asharen are an insectoid species—six-limbed beings standing about four feet tall, with chitinous exoskeletons ranging from deep red to orange-brown. They have compound eyes that see well into the infrared spectrum (useful for their cold nights), mandibles for manipulation and feeding, and the ability to enter suspended animation during extreme cold. They're social creatures, though not as intensely hive-minded as some insectoid species—they maintain individual personalities and autonomy.

The Asharen evolved in underground caverns where geothermal heat kept temperatures survivable. As their population grew, they excavated deeper and expanded horizontally, creating vast subterranean cities. Their architecture is organic—they don't build structures so much as carve them from living rock, creating flowing, curved spaces that feel more grown than constructed.

**Culture and Society:**

Asharen society is organized around **depth-clans**—extended family groups that control specific cavern systems. Social status is determined by depth—the deeper one's clan territory, the warmer and more comfortable, and thus the higher status. The deepest clans are the wealthiest and most powerful, while surface-clans struggle with cold and resource scarcity.

The Asharen have developed a complex theology centered on Sharek, their sister world hanging in the sky. They believe Sharek is the afterlife—when Asharen die, their souls travel across the void to Sharek, where they're reborn. This belief has shaped their entire worldview. They build elaborate towers and platforms on Ashar's surface specifically for death-observations, where the dying are brought to see Sharek one last time before passing. The towers also serve as astronomical observatories.

The Asharen are master astronomers. They've calculated Sharek's exact orbit, mapped its surface features in detail, and theorized extensively about its nature. Some philosophers argue Sharek must be inhabited by the souls of the dead, while others believe it's a paradise world waiting for the Asharen to reach it. This debate has consumed Asharen intellectual life for generations.

**Technology Level:** The Asharen have developed sophisticated engineering—their excavation techniques are advanced, they've invented explosives for mining, and they have metallurgy and chemistry. They're approaching early industrial revolution, having developed primitive steam engines for pumping water and ventilating deep tunnels. However, they have limited experience with surface technology—their ships and vehicles are designed for underground or short surface journeys, not extended exposure.

**Limited Spelljamming:** The Asharen have never achieved spelljamming, but they desperately want it. Reaching Sharek has become the defining goal of their civilization. ## System-Wide Threats and Opportunities

## The Precursor Mystery

Every world in the Tashara Sphere has ruins from the same ancient civilization—a species that seems to have inhabited multiple planets simultaneously, had advanced technology blending magic and engineering, and then vanished completely. The ruins all share architectural features: the black stone that absorbs light, the crystalline data-storage devices, the strange writing system, and evidence of astronomical observation equipment.

The Stardust's sensors could detect patterns invisible to the sphere's current inhabitants—the ruins are all aligned with specific stellar coordinates, as if pointing at something outside the crystal sphere. The data-storage crystals, if properly decoded, might explain why the precursors left or what happened to them. This mystery could drive the entire campaign in the Tashara Sphere, with clues gathered from different worlds gradually revealing the truth.

## Space Hazards

**The Scar-Storms:** The crystal sphere's visible fracture lines occasionally generate phenomenon the Rock of Bral's navigators call "scar-storms"—areas where the sphere's damaged shell leaks energy into wildspace, creating zones of wild magic and gravitational distortion. These storms are unpredictable and can appear anywhere in the system. Ships caught in them experience malfunctioning magic items, bizarre gravitational effects, and occasionally temporal distortions. The storms last anywhere from hours to days.youtube

**Star-Drakes:** The Tashara system is home to a small population of star-drakes—massive draconic creatures that feed on solar radiation by basking near the binary suns. They're not inherently hostile but are territorial, and they view the inner system (Ember-Heart's orbit) as their feeding grounds. Ships that venture too close to the suns may encounter these creatures. They're intelligent enough to communicate but follow their own inscrutable goals. Some Bral traders have successfully negotiated passage through drake territory; others have been destroyed.[spelljammer.fandom](https://spelljammer.fandom.com/wiki/Rock_of_Bral,_The)sj-media-of-wildspace.md.txt

**The Void-Wraiths:** Between the outer planets (Shakti-Monde, Phorvaire, and the Twin Worlds), Bral navigators report occasional encounters with "void-wraiths"—shadowy entities that seem to be native to wildspace itself. They're incorporeal, difficult to detect, and can phase through solid matter. They drain magical energy from spelljamming helms, stranding ships. The wraiths don't seem to be malicious—more like parasites or opportunistic feeders—but they're dangerous nonetheless. The Stardust's magical detection systems might be able to predict their presence.sj-media-of-wildspace.md.txt

## Non-Native Groups

**Bral Trading Posts:** The Rock of Bral has established small, temporary trading posts on several worlds—primarily on Qesh (serving Zh'lani), Vesha-Kai (at the Grandmother Roost), and Tash-Ahn (in the Tash-Vhen city of Keth-Saran). These posts are staffed by a handful of Bral merchants and guards, operating out of fortified compounds. They're resupply points for Rock traders and conduct the bulk of commerce with locals.[spelljammer.fandom+2](https://spelljammer.fandom.com/wiki/Trading_Company,_The)

The trading post operators are valuable sources of information—they know local politics, trade values, and potential dangers. However, they're also competitors. If the Stardust starts trading in the same markets, it could create friction. Some Bral merchants might welcome the company; others might view the Stardust as poaching their territory.

**The Salvagers' Guild:** A small group of independent salvagers has claimed the asteroid ring around Phorvaire as their territory. They're attempting to extract minerals using jury-rigged equipment and converted merchant ships. The operation is dangerous and barely profitable, but they're desperate. They're led by a human captain named **Rivka Tam** (she/her), a former Bral naval officer turned treasure hunter.

Rivka and her crew are potential allies or rivals. If approached respectfully, they might share information about the system or even join forces for specific operations. If the Stardust tries to claim salvage rights without negotiating, Rivka will defend her territory—she has three armed ships and a reputation for ruthlessness.[spelljammer.fandom](https://spelljammer.fandom.com/wiki/Trading_Company,_The)

**The Archeological Expedition:** A team of scholars from the Rock has established a research camp on one of Vesha-Kai's moons (First Eye/The Hunter's Moon), studying the ruins there. They're led by **Professor Malik Chen** (he/him), a gnome archaeologist obsessed with the precursor civilization. His team includes several specialists, guards, and support staff—about twenty people total.sj-media-of-wildspace.md.txt[dumpstatadventures](https://dumpstatadventures.com/the-gm-is-always-right/spelljammer-the-rock-of-bral)

Professor Chen is desperately underfunded and struggling with the challenges of lunar archaeology—equipment failures, dangerous conditions, and mysterious phenomena plaguing the ruins. He'd welcome assistance from the Stardust, either as protection, technical support, or additional expertise. In exchange, he'd share his findings and potentially pay for services.[dumpstatadventures](https://dumpstatadventures.com/the-gm-is-always-right/spelljammer-the-rock-of-bral)sj-media-of-wildspace.md.txt

However, Chen is hiding something. He's discovered evidence suggesting the precursor civilization didn't vanish—they ascended to another plane of existence, and their ruins contain the keys to following them. He's keeping this quiet because he fears others would try to stop him or steal his research. If the Stardust gains his trust, he might reveal his true goal: using the precursor technology to ascend himself.

**The Pilgrims:** A small ship carrying religious pilgrims from various species recently arrived via the Rock of Bral. They believe the Tashara Sphere is somehow sacred—the twin suns, the binary worlds, the visible scars on the crystal sphere all fit prophecies from their diverse faiths. They're led by **Sister Kelan** (she/her), a half-orc priestess who claims to have received visions directing her here.

The pilgrims are peaceful but confused—they're not sure what they're supposed to find or do. They've been exploring the system, visiting different worlds, and conducting religious observances. Some locals find them fascinating; others find them annoying or blasphemous. The pilgrims might hire the Stardust for transport between worlds, or the Stardust might encounter them during their own explorations.

Sister Kelan's visions are genuine—she's unknowingly receiving psychic echoes from the precursor ruins. If this connection is discovered, she could become a valuable asset in decoding the ruins' mysteries, or a liability if the echoes drive her mad.

## Pirates and Hostiles

**The Red Sails:** A small pirate crew operating a captured merchant vessel has been raiding the space between Vesha-Kai and Tash-Ahn, hitting traders traveling between those worlds. They're led by **Captain Soren** (they/them), a half-elf former Bral enforcement officer who turned to piracy after being framed for corruption. Soren's crew is small—maybe fifteen people—but skilled and desperate.[spelljammer.fandom](https://spelljammer.fandom.com/wiki/Trading_Company,_The)

The Red Sails aren't randomly violent—they follow a code of conduct, taking cargo but avoiding casualties when possible. Soren believes they're Robin Hood figures, stealing from exploitative Bral merchants. However, they've hurt plenty of innocent traders, and there's a bounty on their heads from Bral authorities.[spelljammer.fandom](https://spelljammer.fandom.com/wiki/Trading_Company,_The)

The Stardust could encounter the Red Sails as enemies (if transporting valuable cargo), potential allies (if aligned against Bral merchants), or as a rescue opportunity (Soren's crew is slowly falling apart due to internal conflicts).[spelljammer.fandom](https://spelljammer.fandom.com/wiki/Trading_Company,_The)

**The Qeshi Radicals:** A faction of the Free Current Alliance on Qesh has turned to violence in their struggle against Zh'lani's dominance. They're led by **Wind-Caller Tresh** (she/her), a charismatic navigator who believes Zh'lani is destroying the traditional Qeshi way of life. Her followers have begun attacking Zh'lani's sail-walls and raiding merchant convoys.

Tresh's people aren't traditional pirates—they operate on Qesh's surface and in its atmosphere, not in wildspace. However, they'd be hostile to anyone seen as supporting Zh'lani, including potential Bral traders or the Stardust if they negotiate with the Confederation. Conversely, they'd welcome allies who support the Free Current cause.

**The Sharek-Sauresh Conflict:** On Sharek, tensions between the Western Alliance flotillas and several Sauresh city-states have escalated into open warfare. The conflict started over fishing rights but has expanded into a broader struggle over control of coastal territories. Both sides have committed atrocities—the Sharek have blockaded Sauresh ports, and the Sauresh have raided Sharek settlements.

This conflict doesn't directly threaten ships in wildspace, but any vessel visiting Sharek could become entangled. Both sides would try to recruit the Stardust as allies or mercenaries, offering payment for military support. Staying neutral would require careful diplomacy.

## Adventure Seeds and Campaign Structure

## Initial Arrival

The Stardust enters the Tashara Sphere shortly after the Rock of Bral's own arrival, emerging from the Astral Sea into the warm glow of the twin suns. Initial sensor scans reveal a system rich in opportunities but lacking in immediate threats—a welcome change if the crew is weary from previous adventures.

The Rock of Bral is currently in orbit around Qesh, the wind-swept second world. Docking at the Rock would provide opportunities to gather information about the system, meet Bral traders, and hear rumors about various opportunities. The Rock's markets would also allow resupply before venturing deeper into the system.

## Decision Points

From their initial position, Drop Team 6 must decide their approach:

**Option 1 - Follow the Trade Routes:** Work alongside Bral merchants, visiting established trading posts and conducting commerce with known partners. This is safer and more profitable in the short term but offers less opportunity for discovery. They'd primarily visit Qesh, Vesha-Kai, and Tash-Ahn, trading in established markets.

**Option 2 - Pioneer New Contacts:** Visit worlds or species that Bral merchants have limited contact with—the Shakti-Monde civilization, the Khor of Phorvaire, the twin worlds of Ashar and Sharek. This is riskier but potentially more lucrative, as they'd be establishing new trade relationships. It also offers greater opportunities for discovery and exploration.

**Option 3 - Focus on Archaeology:** Partner with Professor Chen's expedition or independently explore the precursor ruins. This offers less immediate profit but potentially invaluable knowledge and access to ancient technology. It also ties into the sphere's overarching mystery.

**Option 4 - Mediation and Diplomacy:** Several conflicts in the system need outside arbitration—the Qeshi city-state tensions, the Sharek-Sauresh war, the Asharen-Sharek first contact situation. Offering diplomatic services could earn gratitude, influence, and long-term relationships with powerful factions.

**Option 5 - Resource Extraction:** Focus on mineral and resource gathering from uninhabited bodies—Ember-Heart's metal rivers, Phorvaire's asteroid ring, the Libration Point between Ashar and Sharek. This requires specialized equipment and involves physical dangers but could be highly profitable.

## Multi-Layered Plots

The beauty of the Tashara Sphere is that every choice opens multiple plot threads:

**Trade in Qesh** could involve:

- Navigating the political tensions between Zh'lani and the Free Current
- Encountering Wind-Caller Tresh's radicals
- Discovering the ancient spelljamming helm beneath Zh'lani
- Racing competitors to secure exclusive trade agreements
- Dealing with void-maw attacks during aerial deliveries

**Exploration on Vesha-Kai** could involve:

- Mediating between Kai-Vesh schools over the Vesh-Kai question
- Assisting with the rocket program to reach the Three Sisters
- Investigating disappearances on Vesha-1
- Studying the mysterious Vesha-3 fungal moon
- Hunting Star-Fangs for bounties or research

**Archaeology on Tash-Ahn** could involve:

- Negotiating access to the mysterious island ruins with the Vhen-Dok
- Facilitating trade between the three species (potential peace-making)
- Dealing with sand beetles, leviathans, and other hazards
- Uncovering the secrets of the underwater ruins
- Exploring Ahn-Kor's crashed ship

**And so on for each world.**

## The Overarching Mystery

As Drop Team 6 explores different worlds and investigates ruins, they'd gradually piece together the precursor civilization's story:

1. **Discovery:** The precursors inhabited multiple worlds simultaneously, suggesting advanced spelljamming or teleportation
2. **Purpose:** All their ruins contain astronomical equipment pointed at specific stellar coordinates outside the sphere
3. **Technology:** Their data-storage crystals contain information about planar mechanics and consciousness transfer
4. **Disappearance:** They didn't die—they collectively ascended to another plane, leaving their physical empire behind
5. **Warning:** The ascension process was dangerous and not entirely successful. Some precursors were lost in the transition, trapped between planes. The void-wraiths encountered in wildspace might be their remnants

This revelation would explain the sphere's mysteries and create a choice: should anyone attempt to follow the precursors' path? Professor Chen certainly wants to try. Sister Kelan's visions might be urging her toward it. Some of the sphere's current inhabitants might be interested if they learned of it.
