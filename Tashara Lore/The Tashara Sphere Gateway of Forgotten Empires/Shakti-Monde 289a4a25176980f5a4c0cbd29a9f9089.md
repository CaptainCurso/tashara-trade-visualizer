# Shakti-Monde

Owner: Nicholas McDowell

Shakti-Monde is a breathtakingly beautiful world of ancient forests, temperate grasslands, and dramatic mountain ranges, blessed with complex ecosystems and two small moons that create mild tidal patterns. The planet's defining characteristic is the remarkable symbiotic relationship between the **Shakti**—an avian species evolved from parrot-like ancestors who stand five feet tall with colorful plumage and vestigial wings—and the **Monde**, large bat-winged mammals roughly horse-sized that serve as their lifelong flying companions. Every Shakti child bonds with a Monde juvenile around age five in a connection so profound that the death of one partner typically leads to the death of the other, and this symbiosis has shaped their entire civilization into an egalitarian, consensus-based society built around tree-cities called roosts that house tens of thousands. The Shakti make decisions through roost-songs—communal singing sessions where every voice is heard and harmony literally represents consensus—and have developed sophisticated late-medieval technology focused on aerial culture, including navigation instruments, lightweight metalworking, and weather prediction. Traders can acquire incredibly valuable Monde-silk (harvested ethically during molting), aerial navigation techniques applicable to spelljamming, bio-medical knowledge about symbiotic relationships, and potentially even the companionship of unbonded Monde for trustworthy crews, though cultural tensions simmer between traditional Forest Roosts and innovative Cliff Roosts over whether ancient songs should evolve with the times.
