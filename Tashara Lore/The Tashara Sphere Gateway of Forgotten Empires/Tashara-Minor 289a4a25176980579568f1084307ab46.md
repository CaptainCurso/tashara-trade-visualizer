# Tashara-Minor

Owner: Nicholas McDowell

Tashara-Minor is the smaller companion of the binary pair, another G-type yellow dwarf with approximately 0.85 to 0.9 solar masses—noticeably dimmer than its sibling but still substantial enough to profoundly influence the system's dynamics. The two suns orbit their common barycenter in a moderately close configuration, maintaining a separation that allows each to retain distinct planetary influences while bathing the entire system in their combined amber glow. Tashara-Minor's slightly cooler surface temperature (around 5,500K compared to Major's 5,800K) gives it a subtly more orange cast that becomes apparent during the dim hours when it alone illuminates the inhabited worlds. Despite being the secondary star, Tashara-Minor is no mere companion—it contributes roughly 40% of the system's total luminosity and creates its own gravitational domain, shepherding asteroids in the outer system and generating complex tidal effects on the inner planets. Together with Tashara-Major, it forms a stable stellar dance that has persisted for billions of years, creating the unique seasonal rhythms and perpetual twilight that define life in this crystal sphere.
