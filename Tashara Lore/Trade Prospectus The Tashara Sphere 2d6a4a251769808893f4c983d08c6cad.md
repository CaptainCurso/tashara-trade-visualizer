# Trade Prospectus: The Tashara Sphere

Owner: Nicholas McDowell

## The Wayfarer’s Compact Trade Prospectus: The Tashara Sphere

## *Compiled 37th Year of Prince Andru's Reign (103 years prior to current date)*

---

**To Our Esteemed Members:**

This prospectus represents months of commerce within the Tashara Sphere during Bral's last sojourn in this sphere. Though conditions may shift in our absence, the fundamental character of markets endures. We remind members that currency adoption fluctuates with political winds—verify local acceptance before assuming Gold Press transactions.

---

## **QESH - The Wind-Swept**

**Primary Settlement:** Zh'lani (Sky-City of the First Current)

**WHAT THEY POSSESS IN ABUNDANCE:**

Breath-Woven textiles (their master-craft), wind-song instruments, pumice stone, preserved kelp, salt, aerodynamic charts. Their weavers produce fabric light as spider-silk yet strong enough for ship rigging.

**WHAT THEY HUNGER FOR:**

Metal of any quality—they possess none naturally. Preserved foods beyond fish. Navigation tools (astrolabes, compasses). Medical supplies for the Weeping Gills (a seasonal affliction among their young). Books and star charts.

**CURRENCY STATUS:** The Confederation of Zh'lani accepted Gold Press during our tenure under Merchant-Navigator Kelesh the Far-Seeing. The Free Current settlements refused coined exchange, insisting on pure barter. *Note: Kelesh fell from power eight years before our departure amid accusations of wind-theft. Verify current authority.*

**HAZARDS:** Void-maws (aerial predators) hunt during their breeding moons. The pumice islands shift routes—do not trust charts older than two seasons.

**CULTURAL NOTE:** The Qeshi practice "wind-debt"—if they guide you through a storm, you owe passage-right in return. Honor this or face embargo.

## **VESHA-KAI - The Cycle's Children**

**Primary Settlements:** Grandmother Roost (forest), High Wind Aerie (mountains)

**WHAT THEY POSSESS IN ABUNDANCE:**

Chitin-forged armor (harvested from their honored dead), agricultural surplus (insect-protein, grains), medicinal herbs, hardwoods, biological specimens. The Vesh-Kai (the small tree-folk) produce exquisite carvings and textiles.

**WHAT THEY HUNGER FOR:**

Metal ores and worked metal. Magical healing (they possess no arcane tradition). Knowledge of other species' death-practices (theological fascination). Communication crystals. Books on alien cultures.

**CURRENCY STATUS:** The School of Patient Growth maintained Gold Press reserves and honored Bral contracts. The School of Sacred Consumption accepted only "meaningful exchange"—goods of cultural significance. The School of the Swift Hunt required demonstration of hunting prowess before trade negotiations. *Note: A schism was brewing over the Vesh-Kai question when we departed. The smaller folk may have achieved different status since.*

**HAZARDS:** Star-Fangs (pack-hunting saurians) in the savannas. Do not hunt without Kai-Vesh guides—violating the Cycle brings swift retribution.

**CULTURAL NOTE:** Ritual cannibalism of their dead is sacred practice. Express no revulsion. When offered chitin-armor, understand you wear someone's ancestor—treat it with reverence.

---

## **TASH-AHN - The Fractured Lands**

**Primary Settlements:** Keth-Saran (desert), Thunderpeak Hold (mountains), Tidecall Harbor (coast)

**THREE PEOPLES, THREE MARKETS:**

**TASH-VHEN (Desert Dwellers):**

*Possess:* Star charts (exceptionally accurate), glass lenses and mirrors (finest available), astronomical instruments, dried provisions, irrigation expertise.

*Desire:* Fresh water sources, metal tools, books, magical items.

*Currency:* Keth-Saran accepted Gold Press. Zur-Malak experimented with water-credit systems. *The Orthodox-Reformed water disputes were escalating when we departed—verify which city survives as primary trade partner.*

**AHN-SHAREK (Mountain Giants):**

*Possess:* Superior metallurgy, exceptional rope, stone crafts, mountain herbs, trained messenger-birds.

*Desire:* Lowland foods (vegetables, grains), writing instruction (oral tradition only), textiles, maps.

*Currency:* No Gold Press acceptance. They trade in "song-debt"—complex obligation system requiring cultural liaison.

**VHEN-DOK (Wave-Riders):**

*Possess:* Preserved fish and salt (high volume), pearls and coral, navigation charts, shipbuilding knowledge.

*Desire:* Corrosion-resistant metals, land-goods they cannot produce, diving equipment.

*Currency:* Major wave-clans accepted Gold Press reluctantly. Preferred material exchange.

**HAZARDS:** Sand beetles in deserts, avalanches in mountains, sea leviathans in coastal waters. The three peoples barely trade with each other—opportunities for intermediaries, but do not take sides in their disputes.

**CULTURAL NOTE:** The Vhen-Dok discovered ancient ruins on a remote island during our stay. They guard its location jealously. Several merchants who pressed too hard vanished at sea.

## **SHAKTI-MONDE - The Bonded Singers**

**Primary Settlement:** Grandmother Roost (largest forest settlement)

**WHAT THEY POSSESS IN ABUNDANCE:**

Monde-silk (shed from their flying companions—extraordinarily light and strong), aerial navigation knowledge (valuable for three-dimensional thinking), song-maps (geographic data in musical form), lightweight metalwork, volcanic-forged goods.

**WHAT THEY HUNGER FOR:**

Metal ores (volcanic supplies insufficient), preserved foods, books and learning materials, communication methods, knowledge of other worlds (intense curiosity).

**CURRENCY STATUS:** They did not comprehend Gold Press during our tenure. Their communal culture operates on gift-economy principles. Offer presents; receive presents. Pressing for "payment" causes offense. *Note: The Cliff Roosts were developing more mercantile attitudes. This may have evolved.*

**HAZARDS:** Shadow-stalkers (six-legged predators) and sky-raptors. The Monde themselves, if their Shakti partners feel threatened—bonded pairs fight as one.

**CULTURAL NOTE:** Never separate bonded Shakti and Monde. Never suggest purchasing a Monde—they are not property. The song-rights between Forest and Cliff Roosts were a matter of growing tension. Avoid taking sides.

## **PHORVAIRE - The Resonant Depths**

**Primary Settlement:** Khorvanum (underground city, one mile deep)

**WHAT THEY POSSESS IN ABUNDANCE:**

Rare minerals found nowhere else, tuned crystals (programmable for communication/navigation), heat-resistant materials, volcanic glass, raw gemstones, geological expertise. Their Resonance Network technology is system-unique.

**WHAT THEY HUNGER FOR:**

Water (any form—worth its weight in platinum), organic materials (they have none), biological knowledge, air-filtration technology, books about carbon-based life.

**CURRENCY STATUS:** The Surface Singers (younger progressive faction) accepted Gold Press and understood merchant principles. The Deep Resonance (traditionalists) refused all currency and most outside contact. The Middle Resonance mediated. *Factional tensions were significant. Verify current authority structure.*

**HAZARDS:** The planet's surface is lethal—volcanic eruptions, toxic gases, earthquakes, crystalline predators. Trade at orbital rendezvous or approved subterranean trade-halls only. Deep Resonance members have attacked surface visitors.

**CULTURAL NOTE:** The Khor communicate through resonance—they literally feel each other's intentions. Deception is nearly impossible with them. Be scrupulously honest or they will cease all contact.

**CRITICAL INTELLIGENCE:** Three merchants who ventured to study ruins on Phorvaire's surface never returned. The Surface Singers claimed volcanic activity. The Deep Resonance smiled (if their crystal faces can be said to smile) and said nothing.

## **ASHAR - The Red Watchers**

**Primary Settlement:** Kelvareth Deep (largest depth-clan territory)

**WHAT THEY POSSESS IN ABUNDANCE:**

Gemstones (significant quantities), excavation technology, explosives, heat-storage materials, astronomical data on their sister-world Sharek, underground construction expertise. *In our final decade, they developed primitive steam engines—this technology may have advanced considerably.*

**WHAT THEY HUNGER FOR:**

**OBSESSIVELY:** Any means of reaching Sharek (their sister world). Spelljamming knowledge, rocket technology, anything enabling space travel. They believe Sharek is the afterlife.

Also: Surface survival equipment, agricultural improvements, medicine for respiratory ailments, confirmation that other inhabited worlds exist.

**CURRENCY STATUS:** Depth-clans possessed substantial Gold Press reserves from historical Bral visits. They will pay extravagant sums for anything advancing their Sharek-reaching goal.

**HAZARDS:** Surface temperatures lethal at night. Underground, getting lost in clan-territories can mean diplomatic incident or death. The Asharen are not hostile but are single-minded about their goal.

**CULTURAL NOTE:** Their death-observation towers on the surface are sacred. Do not land near them. Do not—UNDER ANY CIRCUMSTANCE—reveal that Sharek is inhabited (see below). Such revelation could trigger theological catastrophe.

## **SHAREK - The Blue Mirror**

**Primary Settlements:** Tala-Sharek (floating city, Eastern Ocean), various Sauresh jungle cities

**TWO PEOPLES, COMPLEX RELATIONS:**

**SHAREK (Ocean-Folk):**

*Possess:* Shipbuilding expertise, navigation knowledge, marine resources (fish, pearls, coral), underwater construction techniques, waterproofing materials.

*Desire:* Corrosion-resistant metals, dietary variety, knowledge of Ashar (is it truly land of the dead?), medical supplies for parasites.

*Currency:* The Eastern Flotillas rejected Gold Press (traditional, suspicious). The Western Alliance accepted it cautiously. The Jungle-Walkers (semi-terrestrial minority) served as our primary trade contacts.

**SAURESH (Jungle Dwellers):**

*Possess:* Rare jungle resources (woods, medicinal plants, exotic foods), camouflage materials (color-changing dyes), agricultural knowledge, warrior services (mercenary contracts available).

*Desire:* Better metallurgy, access to ocean resources, weapons and armor, books, military support.

*Currency:* Major city-states maintained Gold Press reserves. Warrior-bands preferred material payment.

**HAZARDS:** Deep-Singers (whale-sized aquatic predators with sonic attacks), jungle apex predators, ongoing warfare between Sharek flotillas and Sauresh city-states. *The conflict when we departed was limited. May have escalated.*

**CULTURAL NOTE:** Like the Asharen, the Sharek believe Ashar is the afterlife (land of the dead). DO NOT reveal it is inhabited. The theological implications would destabilize both worlds. This secret is worth maintaining for stable long-term trade relations.

## **SYSTEM-WIDE NOTES**

**THE PRECURSOR MYSTERY:** Every world bears ruins from an ancient civilization. These contain valuable artifacts but are often dangerous. Local governments may claim salvage rights. The crystal data-storage devices found in these ruins fetch extraordinary prices from scholars on Bral—if you can extract and transport them safely.

**SPACE HAZARDS:** Scar-storms (magical distortions near the sphere's visible cracks), star-drakes (near the twin suns), void-wraiths (in outer system). Standard wildspace precautions apply.

**THE TWIN WORLDS SECRET:** Ashar and Sharek orbit each other, each dominant in the other's sky. Both civilizations believe the other world is the afterlife. Neither knows it is inhabited. Revealing this truth is not recommended—theological chaos on two worlds simultaneously disrupts markets catastrophically. However, facilitating careful first contact could position a merchant house as permanent intermediaries. Assess risk tolerance before attempting.

**OUTDATED INTELLIGENCE (Likely Changed):**

- Queen Sh'lara of Tala-Sharek was elderly when we departed. Succession may have altered Western Alliance politics.
- The Kai-Vesh School of the Final Rest was attracting concerning numbers. This faction may have grown or been suppressed.
- Professor Gavin's archaeological expedition on Vesha-1 was in its third year. They may have completed work or met disaster.
- The Orthodox-Reformed water crisis on Tash-Ahn was approaching war. Resolution unknown.

---

**FINAL GUIDANCE:**

The Tashara Sphere offers substantial opportunity for prepared merchants. Basic sundries (food, fuel, construction materials) are needed by Bral and easily acquired here. Exotic goods (unique textiles, crystals, cultural artifacts) command premium prices in Bral's markets.

The greatest profits lie in intermediary work—these civilizations barely trade with each other. A vessel willing to broker exchanges between worlds can extract commission from all parties.

Verify all political situations upon arrival. One century is sufficient for revolutions, plagues, and philosophical transformations.
