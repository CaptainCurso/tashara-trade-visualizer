# Trade Web of the Tashara Sphere

Owner: Nicholas McDowell

## **The Rock of Bral - Central Trade Hub**

**Role:** Primary currency converter, wholesaler of inter-sphere goods, and only source of Gold Presses in the Tashara Sphere

**What Bral Needs (Basic Sundries):**

- Food: livestock, grains, vegetables, preserved fish, exotic fruits
- Pre-industrial fuel: wood, coal, peat
- Construction materials: timber, stone blocks, metal ores, volcanic glass
- Basic textiles: wool, cotton, leather, rope
- Water and ice (for certain districts)

**What Bral Needs (Exotic Goods for Premium Markets):**

- Magical reagents from unique planetary biomes
- Cultural artifacts and artworks from isolated civilizations
- Unique textiles (Monde-silk, Breath-Woven fabric, bio-luminescent dyes)
- Technological innovations (tuned crystals, Resonance Network components)
- Astronomical data and star charts from isolated systems
- Rare specimens (exotic creatures, plants, minerals)
- Ancient artifacts from precursor ruins
- Services: guides, translators, diplomatic intermediaries

**What Bral Offers:**

- Gold Presses (GP) - the only entity that converts trade goods to standardized currency
- Inter-sphere manufactured goods: books, precision tools, advanced weapons, alchemical supplies
- Magical items and spells from across the galaxy
- Information about the wider galaxy
- Spelljamming vessels, components, and repairs
- Seeds and livestock from other spheres
- Medical knowledge and supplies from advanced civilizations

**Trading Posts:** Maintains small fortified compounds on Qesh (Zh'lani), Vesha-Kai (Grandmother Roost), and Tash-Ahn (Keth-Saran)

## **1. Qesh - The Wind-Sailed World**

**Civilization:** Qeshi (amphibious wind-sailors)

**Population:** ~300,000 across seven major city-states

**Currency:** Limited GP access in Zh'lani; otherwise pure barter

## **What Qeshi Offer:**

**Basic Trade Goods:**

- Breath-Woven Textiles - incredibly light, stronger than steel cable (Confederation monopoly)
- Preserved kelp and seaweed products (staple food, easily preserved)
- Pumice stone (lightweight construction material)
- Salt (from sea-spray harvesting)
- Wind-Song Instruments (unique acoustics, collector's items)

**Exotic Trade Goods:**

- Bio-luminescent dyes (shift color with temperature/emotion)
- Aerodynamic research data (valuable for ship design)
- Wind-prediction techniques (meteorological knowledge)
- Living membrane-sail samples (biological technology)

**Services They'd Trade For:**

- Navigation beyond wind-reading (stellar charts, weather prediction technology)
- Metal working (they'll trade routes/safe passage for smithing services)
- Transport between city-states during storm seasons
- Medical treatment for parasitic disease epidemic
- Diplomatic mediation between Confederation and Free Current Alliance

## **What Qeshi Need:**

**Critical Needs:**

- Metal tools and weapons (they have almost no metallurgy)
- Medical supplies and healing for waterborne parasites
- Preserved foods and spices (their diet is monotonous fish/kelp)
- Writing materials (papyrus, ink, paper)

**Desired Goods:**

- Books from other worlds (they're intellectually curious)
- Navigation instruments (compasses, sextants, astrolabes)
- Specimens from other worlds (for study)
- Better rope/cable than they can manufacture

**Trade Notes:**

- Zh'lani Confederation has GP access and will pay coin for military advantages
- Free Current Alliance will trade exclusively in goods, refusing GP
- Secret: Ancient spelljamming helm beneath Zh'lani worth fortune to right buyer

## **2. Vesha-Kai - The Philosophical Hunters**

**Civilizations:** Kai-Vesh (mantis-folk, carnivorous hunters) & Vesh-Kai (tree-dwelling primates)

**Population:** ~250,000 Kai-Vesh, ~100,000 Vesh-Kai

**Currency:** Some GP in School of Patient Growth territories; otherwise barter

## **What Vesha-Kai Offers:**

**Basic Trade Goods:**

- Chitin-forged armor (lightweight, donated from dead)
- Agricultural innovations (insect farming techniques)
- Biological specimens (extensive ecosystem documentation)
- Hardwoods from ancient forests
- Medicinal herbs and compounds
- Vesh-Kai crafts: textiles, wood carvings, musical instruments

**Exotic Trade Goods:**

- Philosophical texts on ethics, death, and consciousness
- Tracking services (Kai-Vesh are unmatched hunters)
- Kai-Vesh warrior training (combat techniques)
- Ecosystem manipulation knowledge (the Vesh-Kai's "Weaving")
- Sky-glass from Vesha-1 (magical resonance properties)

**Services They'd Trade For:**

- Transport to the Three Sisters moons (obsessive interest)
- Magical healing (they have medicine but no magical tradition)
- Mediation in School disputes over Vesh-Kai rights
- Technology to access Vesha-3's mysteries
- Help excavating/understanding ancient ruins in southern forests

## **What Vesha-Kai Needs:**

**Critical Needs:**

- Metal ores (limited deposits on-world)
- Communication devices for inter-school coordination
- Rocket technology or spelljamming knowledge
- Maps and knowledge of other worlds/species

**Desired Goods:**

- Magical items (they have none)
- Books on alien cultures (philosophical comparison)
- Long-range communication methods
- Mining equipment for moon expeditions
- Medical texts and healing supplies

**Trade Notes:**

- School of Patient Growth has GP and industrial capacity
- Other schools prefer barter and communal exchange
- Vesh-Kai trade separately, often for cultural recognition
- Offering transport to moons could secure exclusive trade agreements

## **3. Tash-Ahn - The Divided World**

**Civilizations:** Tash-Vhen (desert reptilians), Ahn-Sharek (mountain giants), Vhen-Dok (coastal aquatics)

**Population:** ~200,000 Tash-Vhen, ~150,000 Ahn-Sharek, ~300,000 Vhen-Dok

**Currency:** Tash-Vhen cities have GP; others barter

## **What Tash-Ahn Offers:**

**Tash-Vhen (Desert Cities):**

- Star charts and astronomical data (extensive, accurate)
- Glass lenses and mirrors (highest quality in system)
- Astronomical instruments (telescopes, astrolabes)
- Dried foods and preservation techniques
- Irrigation engineering knowledge
- Desert survival equipment

**Ahn-Sharek (Mountain Clans):**

- Superior metal tools and weapons
- Strong rope and cable (mountain-plant fiber)
- Stone carvings and architectural knowledge
- Mountain herbs with unique medicinal properties
- Trained messenger-birds (cliff avians)
- Ore and raw metals

**Vhen-Dok (Coastal Waves):**

- Preserved fish and salt (high quality, high volume)
- Coral and pearls (decorative, magical uses)
- Coastal navigation charts (all coasts mapped)
- Shipbuilding techniques (advanced designs)
- Information about mysterious island ruins

**Services They'd Trade For:**

- Inter-species trade facilitation (they rarely trade with each other)
- Mediating water disputes between Tash-Vhen cities
- Transport across continents
- Help exploring mysterious island ruins
- Knowledge from other worlds
- Technology to reach Ahn-Kor moon

## **What Tash-Ahn Needs:**

**Tash-Vhen:**

- Fresh water sources or water-finding technology
- Metal ores and tools
- Books from other worlds
- Magical knowledge

**Ahn-Sharek:**

- Access to lowland agriculture (food variety)
- Writing systems (they only have oral tradition)
- Lowland trade goods (textiles, preserved foods)
- Maps of the wider system

**Vhen-Dok:**

- Metal tools that resist corrosion
- Diving equipment for deep-ocean exploration
- Help accessing island ruins
- Defense against land-based threats

**Trade Notes:**

- Orthodox Tash-Vhen (Keth-Saran) will pay GP, Reformed (Zur-Malak) prefers barter
- Ahn-Sharek trade in goods and song-debts (cultural obligation)
- Vhen-Dok use wave-clan reputation as currency alongside goods
- Serving as inter-species broker is highly profitable - they want each other's goods

## **4. Shakti-Monde - The Aerial Symbiotes**

**Civilization:** Shakti (flightless avians) bonded with Monde (flying mammals)

**Population:** ~530,000 Shakti across four cultural regions

**Currency:** Minimal GP understanding; communal exchange preferred

## **What Shakti-Monde Offers:**

**Basic Trade Goods:**

- Monde-silk (shed naturally, incredibly light and strong)
- Lightweight metalworking (frames, tools, specialized techniques)
- Forest and jungle resources (sustainable harvesting)
- Volcanic forge access (mountain regions)
- Medicinal knowledge of symbiotic biology

**Exotic Trade Goods:**

- Aerial navigation techniques (3D spatial awareness, invaluable for spelljamming)
- Song-maps (geographic information encoded in songs)
- Trained Monde (non-bonded juveniles, rare honor)
- Bio-medical treatments for bonding-related ailments
- Ecosystem management knowledge
- First Eye aluminum samples (if retrieved from moon)

**Services They'd Trade For:**

- Medical help for Monde epidemic (critical need)
- Mediation in Forest Roosts vs. Cliff Roosts song-rights dispute
- Transport to the Twin Eyes moons (intense curiosity)
- Education about other worlds and species
- Help accessing forbidden ancient ruins beneath Grandmother Roost
- Teaching of writing systems (they use oral tradition)

## **What Shakti-Monde Needs:**

**Critical Needs:**

- Medicine for Monde epidemic (desperate)
- Metal ores for volcanic forges
- Preserved foods for harsh-winter regions
- Outside mediator for song-rights dispute

**Desired Goods:**

- Books and learning materials (highly valued)
- Knowledge of other worlds
- Specimens from alien biomes
- Communication technology
- Navigation instruments

**Trade Notes:**

- Communal culture makes negotiation different - offer gifts, receive gifts
- Helping with epidemic grants access to otherwise forbidden locations
- Trained Monde are incredibly valuable but require deep trust
- Song-maps are trade secrets but might be shared for sufficient friendship

## **5. Phorvaire - The Crystal World**

**Civilization:** Khor (silicon-based crystalline beings)

**Population:** ~250,000 across three resonance-frequency groups

**Currency:** Surface Singers understand GP; Deep Resonance refuses it; Middle Resonance negotiates

## **What Phorvaire Offers:**

**Basic Trade Goods:**

- Rare mineral samples (elements found nowhere else)
- Heat-resistant materials (survive extreme temperatures)
- Volcanic glass and obsidian (high quality, large quantities)
- Raw gems and crystals (mining byproduct)

**Exotic Trade Goods:**

- Tuned Crystals (programmable resonance for communication/navigation/weapons)
- Resonance Network technology (instant communication system)
- Geological survey services (sense ore deposits by resonance)
- Crystal art (hauntingly beautiful sculptures)
- Materials science knowledge (heat resistance, crystal growth)
- Volcanic prediction techniques

**Services They'd Trade For:**

- Information about organic life/biology (they have zero experience)
- Water (pure water extremely valuable)
- Mediation between Deep Resonance and Surface Singers
- Help accessing ancient ruins protected by immunity field
- Transport to asteroid ring (they've failed repeatedly with rockets)
- Translation of precursor ruin data-crystals

## **What Phorvaire Needs:**

**Critical Needs:**

- Water (any form, any purity)
- Organic materials (they have almost none)
- Air-filtration technology for surface installations
- Knowledge of biology and medicine

**Desired Goods:**

- Books and data from other worlds
- Living specimens from carbon-based biomes
- Wood, plants, biological samples
- Food items (to study, not eat)

**Trade Notes:**

- Deep Resonance are isolationist, hostile to outsiders
- Surface Singers are eager to trade but lack authority
- Middle Resonance can broker deals if convinced of mutual benefit
- Tuned Crystals and Resonance Network tech are system-unique and valuable across galaxy
- Planet surface is extremely dangerous - trading at orbital rendezvous recommended

- Surface survival equipment (heating, insulation)

## **What Asharen Need:**

**Critical Needs:**

- Food production improvements

## **8. The Asharen-Sharek Service Opportunity**

**The Ultimate Service Trade:**

The Asharen will pay enormous sums to be transported to Sharek to "experience the afterlife." The Sharek, meanwhile, are increasingly curious about whether Ashar is truly the land of the dead and might pay to visit.

**What Asharen Would Trade for Sharek Transport:**

- Entire clan treasuries (thousands of GP)
- Exclusive mining rights on Ashar
- Gemstone shipments (regular supply contracts)
- Steam engine technology transfer
- Explosive formulae
- Deep-clan political support

**What Sharek Would Trade for Ashar Transport:**

- Access to underwater ruins and their artifacts
- Shipbuilding secrets
- Exclusive trade routes through their waters
- Marine resource harvesting rights
- Deep Mother temple blessings (cultural significance)

**Complications:**

- Both civilizations would experience profound theological shock
- Revealing the truth could destabilize both societies
- Could create unprecedented cultural exchange or devastating conflict
- Potential for long-term brokerage role between twin worlds

## **Optimal Trade Routes for the Stardust**

## **Route 1: The Basics Circuit (Low Risk, Steady Profit)**

1. **Qesh → Tash-Ahn**: Trade Breath-Woven textiles and instruments for Ahn-Sharek metals and Tash-Vhen glass
2. **Tash-Ahn → Vesha-Kai**: Trade metals and glass for chitin-armor and agricultural innovations
3. **Vesha-Kai → Shakti-Monde**: Trade metal goods for Monde-silk and lightweight metalwork
4. **Shakti-Monde → Bral**: Convert exotic textiles, instruments, and crafts to GP

**Profit Margin:** Moderate (200-300% markup at Bral)

**Risk:** Low (established routes, peaceful civilizations)

**Time:** 4-6 weeks for circuit

## **Route 2: The Crystal Connection (High Value, Moderate Risk)**

1. **Phorvaire → Multiple Worlds**: Trade Tuned Crystals and Resonance Network components
    - Qeshi pay premium for communication crystals
    - Asharen desperate for any advanced technology
    - Sharek interested in underwater communication
2. **Multiple Worlds → Phorvaire**: Bring water (critical), organic samples, and biological knowledge
3. **Phorvaire → Bral**: Convert unique crystals and heat-resistant materials to GP

**Profit Margin:** High (400-600% markup at Bral for unique tech)

**Risk:** Moderate (planet surface dangerous, Deep Resonance hostile)

**Time:** 2-3 weeks per transaction

## **Route 3: The Mediation Service (Complex, Maximum Profit)**

**Phase 1 - Inter-Species Brokerage on Tash-Ahn:**

- Facilitate Tash-Vhen ← → Ahn-Sharek ← → Vhen-Dok trade
- Tash-Vhen get metals, Ahn-Sharek get food, Vhen-Dok get glass/tools
- Take 10-15% commission on all trades
- Build reputation and goodwill

**Phase 2 - Lunar Expeditions:**

- Transport Kai-Vesh scientists to Three Sisters
- Transport Asharen explorers to Ahn-Kor
- Recover valuable materials and artifacts
- Charge for transportation + share of discoveries

**Phase 3 - The Twin Worlds Revelation:**

- Carefully orchestrate Asharen-Sharek first contact
- Position as essential intermediaries
- Establish permanent brokerage role
- Long-term profit from all inter-world trade

**Profit Margin:** Extreme (1000%+ through commissions and exclusive access)

**Risk:** High (political, theological, logistical complications)

**Time:** Months to establish, permanent ongoing income

## **Route 4: The Expedition Support (Opportunistic)**

**Services for Non-Native Groups:**

- Supply runs for Professor Chen's archaeological expedition
- Escort for Sister Kelan's pilgrims
- Competition with Rivka Tam's Salvagers' Guild (or partnership)
- Anti-piracy work against Red Sails (bounty from Bral)

**Profit Margin:** Variable (depends on contracts)

**Risk:** Variable

**Time:** Flexible

## **Summary: Strategic Trade Web**

**Hub Role:** Rock of Bral converts all goods to GP - the Stardust's ultimate goal is always to return with cargo valuable to Bral's markets.

**Basic Sundries Flow:**

- Food: Qesh (kelp/fish) → Bral
- Wood: Shakti-Monde, Vesha-Kai → Bral
- Metal: Ahn-Sharek, Phorvaire → Bral
- Stone: Tash-Vhen, Vhen-Dok → Bral

**Exotic Goods Flow:**

- Textiles: Qesh (Breath-Woven), Shakti-Monde (Monde-silk), Sharek (camouflage) → Bral → Premium prices
- Technology: Phorvaire (crystals), Asharen (steam engines), Shakti-Monde (navigation) → Bral → Collectors/military
- Cultural items: All worlds → Bral → Museums, scholars, collectors
- Knowledge: Tash-Vhen (astronomy), Vesha-Kai (philosophy), Khor (geology) → Bral → Academic market
- Magical items: Ancient ruins across system → Bral → Highest premium

**Service Opportunities:**

- Medical intervention (Shakti-Monde epidemic)
- Diplomatic mediation (Qesh, Vesha-Kai, Tash-Ahn, Sharek internal conflicts)
- Transportation (especially to moons and between twin worlds)
- Protection (escort, anti-piracy, guards)
- Exploration (ruins, moons, dangerous locations)
- First contact facilitation (Asharen-Sharek)

**Competitive Landscape:**

- Bral merchants control established routes (Qesh, Vesha-Kai, Tash-Ahn)
- Untapped markets: Shakti-Monde, Phorvaire, full Sharek access
- Hidden opportunities: Ancient ruins, moon expeditions, precursor mystery
- Rival/Partner: Rivka Tam's salvagers in asteroid belt

**Optimal Strategy:**

1. Start with Route 1 (safe capital building)
2. Develop relationships through service contracts
3. Transition to Route 2 (high-value unique goods)
4. Establish Route 3 (long-term brokerage empire)
5. Throughout: opportunistically pursue ruins, artifacts, and expedition support

The Tashara Sphere offers a self-contained economy where the Stardust can bootstrap from simple cargo runs to becoming indispensable intermediaries across an entire star system - all flowing toward the ultimate goal of converting exotic goods to Gold Presses at Bral's markets.
